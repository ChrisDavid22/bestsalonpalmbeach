What Makes a "Best" Colorist?

Not all colorists are created equal. Here's what separates the best from the rest:

1. Brand Certifications

Top colorists invest in professional certifications from major brands like Davines, Goldwell, L'Oreal, Redken, and Wella. Each certification represents months of specialized training.

2. Years of Color-Specific Experience

General styling experience is different from color expertise. The best colorists have 10-20+ years focusing specifically on color services.

3. Educator Status

Many elite colorists are selected by brands to train other professionals. Being an educator means the industry recognizes their expertise.

4. Correction Ability

Anyone can apply color to virgin hair. The true test is fixing disasters - brassy tones, box dye removal, uneven color.

Price vs. Quality Considerations

The cheapest option isn't always the best value.

A poorly executed color correction can cost $300-800 to fix. An inexperienced blonde job can damage your hair for months. Investing in quality the first time often saves money long-term.

That said, expert colorists command premium prices for a reason. For simple services, a mid-tier salon may be perfectly adequate.
